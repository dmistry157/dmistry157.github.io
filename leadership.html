Access Books Fundraiser

View this post on Instagram

A post shared by Access Books (@accessbooksca)

Fundraiser: Raised $14000 for Dolores Huerta Elementary School, in an underserved community in South Los Angeles. These funds were put towards buying a new set of books, amounting to over 1300 hardcovers of high interest to the students. Reading at a young age is one of the largest determiners of one's literacy later in life. I raised these funds by applying for city grants and spreading the word around my community, such as giving talks at school, at different community service organizations I was a part of, and going door-to-door posting flyers in small businesses.

Book Drive: Raised 1500+ new and gently used books for grades K-5. Some were sorted and labelled for teachers to keep in their classrooms and for kids to take home. These came from local elemntary schools in Thousand Oaks, publishing companies, and mostly from individual donors.

Event: On May 4th, 2024, a group of 50 volunteers came to sort, label, and catalogue over 2800 books for Dolores Huerta Elementary's new library. Prior to the event, we weeded out old, outdated books, some of which were rotting. Through the services of this event, the fundraiser, book drive, and prior work(ordering the books, weeding out old books, and organizing the library, which I had extensive help from the Access Books team), we saved the school roughly $80,000 covering materials, labor, taxes, and shipping.

Varsity Tennis Captain

In my senior year I am leading the Varsity tennis team at my high school, nominated by the varsity team members.

I was placed on varsity my sophomore year, and I have helped teach incoming JV teammates, design new uniforms, and lead at practices and matches.

I aim to keep my team motivated, and I am usually the loudest cheerer on and off the court. I even got the best sportsmanship award on the team for this in 2024.

I play No. 1 doubles, last year ranking 5th in the Maramonte League.

Math Help Tutoring

At Westlake HS, I teach through Math Help, a free environment for math tutoring across all math classes at lunch. At colina I teach through SMART tutoring, an after-school program for students that are struggling with their grades or the content they are learning.

Over the last 4 years I have spent roughly 200 hours tutoring math across multiple subjects, different ages, and at different experience levels.

I know how to explain the same thing in multiple ways, which allows me to teach many types of students as everyone learns differently. I aim to connect with students to help them with their academic goals, not just their assignments.

The subjects that I help people with includes 6th-8th grade math, Geometry, Algebra 1, Algebra 2, Pre Calculus, and Calculus.

Assisteens

I have been part of Assisteens for 3 years now. I helped run the Thrift Shop, make baskets and gift boxes for underprivileged families, support families at Safe Passage, and make lunches and dinners for the homeless through Harbor House.

At the meetings and special events, we make gift baskets to be distributed by other community service organizations.

The thrift shop takes in donations from the community and organizes the goods to resell them for incredibly low prices. I worked at the register at the thrift shop handling transactions and bagging/wrapping.

Safe Passage provides food, supplies, and resources to anyone who needs it in Conejo Valley. I volunteered in the part that distributed food and supplies. We would go to a neighborhood and set up a supply line and people could come through to pick up whatever groceries or supplies that they needed for free. I helped to hand out food to people going through the line and carry food/supplies back to their houses if they were physically unable.

Harbor House provides free meals to the homeless community. I would make lunches and dinners for Harbor House so that they could give it to people who would otherwise be unable to afford it.

In the photo I am making 25 dinners for Harbor House.
